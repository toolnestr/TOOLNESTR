# 01 — Market Research & Strategy

This is the "why this will work" file. Everything below is based on real data gathered
during planning (June 2026).

## 1. Tool/calculator pages are organic-traffic machines

People type their exact problem into Google ("age calculator", "20 percent off", "json
formatter"). A single tool page that answers it can pull enormous, *free, recurring*
search traffic. Real examples found during research:

| Tool page | Monthly organic visits | Why it works |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------|
| SIP Calculator (Groww) | ~3,500,000 | One page, one job |
| Percentage Calculator | ~1,600,000 | Simple, universal need |
| Mortgage Calculator (Bankrate) | ~1,400,000 | High-value, repeated need |
| Paycheck Calculator (SmartAsset) | ~690,000 | Programmatic: one page **per US state** |
| Calorie Calculator (Mayo Clinic) | ~456,000 | Trusted, simple |

Big multi-tool sites prove the model: **calculator.net gets ~58 million visits/month**,
~51% from Google organic. Sites like basiccalculatoronline.com report 10M+ users/month.

**Takeaway:** We don't need one viral tool. We need ~30 decent tools each pulling
1,500–5,000 visits/month → that's our 50k/month, and it compounds as we add tools.

## 2. How we beat competition (the long-tail rule)

The head term "percentage calculator" is owned by giant sites. We do **not** fight there
head-on. We win with **long-tail + niche + programmatic** pages:

- Instead of just "discount calculator" → also "20 percent off calculator",
  "buy one get one calculator", "double discount calculator".
- Instead of "tax calculator" → "GST calculator" pages per country/rate.
- Instead of competing with dev giants → niche dev tools devs still hunt for.
- Creator tools (caption/hashtag/fancy-text/engagement) are trendy and **less SEO-saturated**
  than classic calculators → faster to rank, very shareable (social traffic too).

**The pattern of a real low-competition opportunity:** people repeatedly ask "is there a
tool that does X?" and get no good answer. Our job is to *be* that answer.

## 3. Why three audiences (mix) is smart here

You chose a mix of everyday/students + creators + developers. That's deliberate:

- **Everyday/students** = highest, most stable search volume + best AdSense pay (finance,
  education niches pay well).
- **Creators** = viral/shareable, high time-on-site (good for ad views), social traffic.
- **Developers** = genuinely low competition + easy/cheap to build (all run in the browser).

Spreading across three niches means if one niche is slow to rank, the others carry traffic.

## 4. Monetization (future)

- **Primary:** Google AdSense (display ads). Tool sites are AdSense-friendly because pages
  are useful, original, and get real search traffic.
- **AdSense needs (2026):** ~20–30 quality pages, About + Privacy Policy + Contact pages,
  HTTPS, mobile-friendly, original content. A 30-tool site + short guide articles meets this.
- **No fixed traffic minimum** for approval — quality + trust pages matter more. We apply in
  Phase 3 once we have ~15+ solid tool pages and the trust pages.
- **Later add-ons:** affiliate links (relevant tools/services), premium/no-ad tier, sponsor
  slots. We design now so ads slot in cleanly later without hurting speed.

## 5. Realistic timeline to 50k/month

SEO is a slow build, not a switch. Honest expectation:

| Month | Tools live | Expected visits/mo |
|-------|-----------|--------------------|
| 1–2 | 8–10 | a few hundred (Google indexing) |
| 3–4 | 15–20 | 2,000–8,000 |
| 5–8 | 25–30 | 10,000–30,000 |
| 9–12 | 30 + articles | 40,000–60,000+ |

Drivers that speed this up: more tools, supporting articles, internal links between tools,
shares on Reddit/forums, and clean technical SEO (which Astro gives us).
