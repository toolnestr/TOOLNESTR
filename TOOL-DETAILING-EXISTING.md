## scientific-calculator
**Intro:** A scientific calculator handles far more than basic arithmetic —
trigonometry, logarithms, exponents, roots, and constants like π and e. It's the
everyday tool for students, engineers, and anyone working beyond +, −, ×, ÷.
This calculator runs in your browser with full order-of-operations support, so
complex expressions evaluate correctly without manual grouping mistakes.

**How it works:** It parses your expression following standard order of
operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS): parentheses, exponents, multiplication/division,
then addition/subtraction. Functions like `sin`, `cos`, `log`, `ln`, and `√`
apply to the value or bracket that follows them.

**Worked example:** `3 + 4 × 2² = 3 + 4 × 4 = 3 + 16 = 19` (exponent first, then
multiply, then add).

**Use cases:** Algebra and physics homework · engineering calculations ·
quick trig/log evaluation · checking results from a phone calculator.

**Tips & mistakes:** Wrap negatives in parentheses, e.g. `(-3)²` vs `-3²`. Check
whether trig is in degrees or radians before trusting the result.

**FAQ:**
- *What's the order of operations?* Parentheses → exponents → multiply/divide →
  add/subtract, left to right.
- *Does it do radians or degrees?* Set the mode before using trig functions;
  the same input gives different results in each.
- *Can it handle long expressions?* Yes — it parses the full expression at once,
  so you don't need to calculate step by step.

## fraction-calculator
**Intro:** Fractions trip people up because you can't just add the tops and
bottoms. This calculator adds, subtracts, multiplies, and divides fractions and
returns the answer in lowest terms, so you get a clean, correct result every
time — including mixed numbers.

**How it works:** For + and −, it finds a common denominator, combines the
numerators, then simplifies using the greatest common divisor (GCD). For × it
multiplies straight across; for ÷ it multiplies by the reciprocal.

**Worked example:** `1/2 + 1/3 = 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6`. `2/3 ÷ 4/5 = 2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6`.

**Use cases:** School math · cooking/recipe scaling · woodworking and
measurements · simplifying ratios.

**Tips & mistakes:** Don't add denominators — find a common one first. Convert
mixed numbers (e.g. 1½) to improper fractions before calculating.

**FAQ:**
- *How do I add fractions with different denominators?* Convert both to a common
  denominator, add the numerators, then simplify.
- *What's an improper fraction?* One where the numerator is larger than the
  denominator (e.g. 7/4); it can be written as a mixed number.
- *Does it simplify the answer?* Yes — results are reduced to lowest terms.

## average-calculator
**Intro:** The average (mean) summarises a set of numbers in a single value. This
calculator takes any list of numbers and returns the mean — and often the median,
mode, sum, and count — so you can describe a data set at a glance.

**How it works:** The mean is the sum of all values divided by how many there
are. The median is the middle value when sorted; the mode is the most frequent.

**Worked example:** Numbers 4, 8, 15, 16, 23, 42 → sum 108 ÷ 6 = **18 average**.

**Use cases:** Class grades · test scores · expenses · any quick data summary.

**Tips & mistakes:** A few extreme values skew the mean — check the median too.
Separate your numbers consistently (commas or spaces) so none are missed.

**FAQ:**
- *What's the difference between mean and median?* Mean is the arithmetic
  average; median is the middle value — less affected by outliers.
- *Can I average decimals?* Yes — mix whole numbers and decimals freely.
- *What is the mode?* The value that appears most often in the list.

## standard-deviation
**Intro:** Standard deviation measures how spread out a set of numbers is around
its average. A low value means the data is tightly clustered; a high value means
it's widely scattered. This calculator returns the standard deviation, variance,
mean, and count for any data set.

**How it works:** It finds the mean, then the squared difference of each value
from the mean, averages those (variance), and takes the square root. It supports
both population and sample formulas (the sample version divides by n−1).

**Worked example:** For 2, 4, 4, 4, 5, 5, 7, 9 → mean 5, population SD = 2.

**Use cases:** Statistics homework · quality control · finance/risk · comparing
the consistency of two data sets.

**Tips & mistakes:** Choose sample (n−1) when your data is a sample of a larger
group; use population (n) only for the entire group. Mixing them is a common error.

**FAQ:**
- *Population vs sample standard deviation?* Population divides by n; sample
  divides by n−1 to correct bias when estimating from a sample.
- *What does a high standard deviation mean?* The values are spread far from the
  average — more variability.
- *What's variance?* The square of the standard deviation.

**Related tools:** Average Calculator · Scientific Calculator · Random Number Generator

- Description: Calculate standard deviation, variance, and mean for any data set, with both population and sample formulas. Free statistics calculator.

## percentage-calculator
**Intro:** Percentages appear everywhere — discounts, tips, grades, growth. This
calculator answers the three common percentage questions: what is X% of Y, X is
what percent of Y, and percentage increase or decrease between two numbers.

**How it works:** "X% of Y" multiplies Y by X/100. "X is what % of Y" divides X
by Y and multiplies by 100. Percentage change is the difference divided by the
original, times 100.

**Worked example:** 20% of 150 = 150 × 0.20 = **30**. Change from 80 to 100 =
(100−80)/80 × 100 = **+25%**.

**Use cases:** Discounts and sales · tips · test scores · profit and growth rates.

**Tips & mistakes:** Percentage increase then the same percentage decrease does
NOT return the original (e.g. +50% then −50% ≠ start). Always divide change by
the original value, not the new one.

**FAQ:**
- *How do I find what percent one number is of another?* Divide the part by the
  whole and multiply by 100.
- *How do I calculate percentage increase?* (New − Old) ÷ Old × 100.
- *Why isn't +20% then −20% back to the start?* Each percent applies to a
  different base, so they don't cancel.

**Related tools:** Discount Calculator · Fraction Calculator · Average Calculator
**SEO meta:**
- Title: Percentage Calculator — Percent Of, Change & Ratio
- Description: Calculate X% of a number, what percent one number is of another, and percentage increase or decrease. Fast, free percentage calculator.

## compound-interest
**Intro:** Compound interest is interest earned on both your original money and
the interest it has already earned — the engine of long-term saving and
investing. This calculator projects how a balance grows over time, optionally
with regular contributions, and splits the result into deposits versus growth.

**How it works:** It applies the rate over each compounding period (yearly,
monthly, daily) and, if set, adds your recurring contribution each period, then
compounds the new total.

**Worked example:** $1,000 at 7%/yr, 10 years, compounded monthly →
1000 × (1 + 0.07/12)^120 ≈ **$2,009.66**, of which ~$1,009.66 is interest.

**Use cases:** Retirement projections · savings planning · comparing accounts ·
showing the value of starting early.

**Tips & mistakes:** More frequent compounding grows slightly faster. Remember
inflation reduces the real value of future money.

**FAQ:**
- *Simple vs compound interest?* Simple pays only on the principal; compound pays
  on principal plus accumulated interest, so it grows faster.
- *Does it include contributions?* Yes — add a recurring amount to model regular
  saving.
- *Are taxes and inflation included?* No — it shows gross growth.

**Related tools:** Investment Calculator · Inflation Calculator · Salary Calculator

repayments · checking lender quotes.

- *What is EMI?* A fixed monthly payment covering both principal and interest.
- *How does tenure affect EMI?* Longer tenure = smaller EMI but more total
  interest.
- *Is the rate monthly or yearly?* Enter the annual rate; the formula converts it

- *How do I lower my break-even point?* Cut fixed costs, raise price, or reduce
  variable cost per unit.

## roas-calculator
**Intro:** ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) measures revenue generated for every unit of
currency spent on advertising. This calculator finds your ROAS and helps judge
whether a campaign is profitable.

**How it works:** ROAS = revenue from ads ÷ ad spend. A ROAS of 4 means $4 back
for every $1 spent. It's often shown as a ratio or percentage.

**Worked example:** $2,000 revenue from $500 ad spend → 2000 / 500 = **4× ROAS**

**Use cases:** Evaluating ad campaigns · comparing channels · setting budgets ·
deciding whether to scale ads.

**Tips & mistakes:** ROAS ignores product cost — a high ROAS can still lose money
if margins are thin. Compare ROAS to your break-even ROAS (1 ÷ margin).

**FAQ:**
- *What's a good ROAS?* It depends on margins; a common rule of thumb is 3–4×, but
  thin-margin products need more.
- *ROAS vs ROI?* ROAS measures revenue per ad dollar; ROI accounts for all costs
  and profit.
- *What is break-even ROAS?* 1 ÷ profit margin — the ROAS where ads neither make
  nor lose money.

## base64
**Intro:** Base64 encodes text or binary data into a safe ASCII string for use in
URLs, JSON, emails, and data URIs — and decodes it back. This tool converts both
ways, entirely in your browser.

**How it works:** Encoding maps every 3 bytes to 4 ASCII characters from the
Base64 alphabet; decoding reverses it. Unicode is handled via UTF-8.

**Worked example:** "Hello" → `SGVsbG8=` and back to "Hello".

**Use cases:** Decoding config/API values · building data URIs · debugging
encoded payloads · embedding small assets.

**Tips & mistakes:** Base64 is encoding, not encryption — it hides nothing. It
increases size by ~33%.

**FAQ:**
- *Is Base64 secure?* No — it's reversible and readable by anyone; it's not
  encryption.
- *Why use it?* To carry binary or special characters safely through text-only
  channels.
- *Is my data uploaded?* No — encoding/decoding happens in your browser.

**Related tools:** JSON Formatter · Number Base Converter · Password Generator
**SEO meta:**
- Title: Base64 Encoder / Decoder — Encode & Decode Online
- Description: Encode text to Base64 or decode Base64 to text instantly, with Unicode support. Runs in your browser — nothing is uploaded.
